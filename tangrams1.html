Part 1A: Introduction

Tangram is an ancient Chinese puzzle where players arrange geometrically shaped pieces into a particular design by flipping, rotating, and moving them. In this lab, you will be playing an online game, Tangrams. In this game, you will have the opportunity to design many versions of the original game, and you will play the role of a statistician as you design a study, collect data, and then analyze and interpret the results. Your goal is to arrange the geometric shapes on the left, called tans, into a specific pattern or design on the right as quickly as possible.

Part 1B: Playing Tangrams

In Tangrams, you need to move the geometirc shapes on the left (tans) into a specific pattern or design on the right.

Controls:
  • Move the Mouse to drag the geometirc shapes on the left (tans) to the design on the right.
  • Click the Rotate Left Button to rotate the shape counterclockwise.
  • Click the Flip Button to flip the shape.
  • Click the Rotate Right Button to rotate the shape clockwise.
  • Click Hint see a hint.
  • Click Reset to reset the shape back to the left panel.
  • Click Quit to exit the game and return to the main menu.

    • Goal: Arrange the geometric shapes on the left, called tans, into a specific pattern or design on the right as quickly as possible.

    Play:
  • Player ID The game will ask you for a Player ID. You can use any name you like, but this will be on the internet, so do not use a name that will easily identify you.
  • Group ID If you are playing this game for a course, your instructor will provide a Group ID so you will be able to compete with everyone in your class.
  • Click Select Puzzle Type
  • Choose Time Limit
  • Click Start
    • Note: If you are unable to play the game, please try a different browser or go to the game website directly Here.

    Part 1E: Collect Your Own Data

    Let's conduct a hypothesis test about students in our course. (e.g. on average can students complete a particular puzzle in less than ___ minutes?)

      1. State the null and alternative hypotheses corresponding to the objective of this study.
      2. All students in the class should play the game. Each student will be considered a subject in this study. In this test, we are simply conducting a test on game completion time. However, we want to make the conditions as similar as possible for all subjects. Determine the settings for each of the following variables on the Tangram:
      • Game
      • Time Limit
      • Show Timer
      • Hints Allowed
      • Puzzle Type
      3. List any other conditions that should be controlled during this study. After the settings/conditions have been defined, each student should play the game one time.
      4. Why is it important to attempt to ensure that all subjects play the game under the same conditions?
      5. After all students complete the game, use the Recorded Data button to view all data from our study (use same Group ID as above). Copy and paste the data into Minitab, Excel, or other statistical software package. Create a histogram or box plot of your data Identify any outliers or skewness shown in the plot. Identify any potential errors in the data (use the player alias to identify specific data). Correct or delete the erroneous data. Briefly explain any changes you made to the data set and describe why you made these changes.
      6. Use appropriate statistical techniques to calculate a p-value for your study.
      7. What assumptions need to be checked before we can conclude the analysis in Question 6 is appropriate? For example, did keeping or removing the outliers influence the results? If some of the students used hints and others did not, are the results biased?
      8. State your conclusions in context. For example, explain whether we can use this data to conclude that our results hold for all students at our school. In addition, if the model assumptions were not perfectly met, explain how your results might be influenced.
      9. Create a 95% confidence interval for the mean, use the form (a, b) where a represents the lower bound and b represents the upper bound.
      10. Identify which of the following statements are proper interpretations of a confidence interval. If a statement is not correct, explain why.
        a) We are 95% confident that the true average time it takes students to complete the Tangrams game is in the interval (a, b).
        b) We are 95% certain that each student takes approximately a to b seconds to complete the Tangrams game.
        c) We expect 95% of the students to have completion times between a and b seconds.
        d) We expect about 95% of all possible sample means from this population to be between a and b seconds.

    Part 1F: Get Curious

    1. If you had a chance to play the game under different circumstances, would you be able to perform better? Describe any factors that may have kept you from doing your best while playing the Tangrams game.

    2. Do you think outside factors (like the ones you or others mentioned in the previous question) could impact the results of a study? Explain why or why not. For example, should researchers provide some type of motivation for their subjects in various studies to do their best?

    3. If you were conducting this study again, how would you control for any key factors that may influence each subject's performance?
