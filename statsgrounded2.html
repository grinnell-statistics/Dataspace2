Introduction

Your friend Joe runs a small coffee business where he has his own Coffee truck that he inherited from his uncle. He needs your help to determine the best location to sell coffee (maximize his profit). He has daily expenses of $300 and each cup of coffee costs about $0.50 to make. To figure out where he should sell, you will be using an online coffee game to simulate his sales at two different locations.

Step 1: Collecting Data

The game can be played below. Before you start, if you are doing this in a classroom setting, please check Save data box and input the Group Name for the class provided by your instructor. Then, you will also input a Player Name. This will be on the internet, so do not use a name that will easily identify or is inappropriate. Joe is wondering whether the Business District or City Park is the best place to sell. At both places, he will sell a cup for $3 with no music during lunch time. Choose the following settings as you see them below. Next, you will want to run 10 repetitions of each scenario where the Design Type is Random Sample. Then click Start Sim.

Once you start sim, you can then select Get All Sales Data and then Game Data.
